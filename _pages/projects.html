Master's Thesis

A Machine Learning Approach for Evaluating Climate Sensitivity of Infectious Diseases in Refugee Communities

Abstract: Globalization, climate change, and increased population mobility have intensified the challenges of infectious disease outbreaks for global public health, as many infectious pathogen's life cycles exhibit strong climate sensitivity. Refugee communities are particularly vulnerable to contagious diseases due to overcrowding, inadequate sanitation infrastructure, and delayed healthcare access. Accurate predictions of disease dynamics can help public health organizations plan interventions to reduce transmission.

However, traditional time-series methods and reporting surveillance systems fail to detect complex patterns from multiple features in infectious disease forecasting in refugee communities, where data scarcity and crisis-specific factors make reliable predictions difficult. This study develops and validates a robust, interpretable machine learning framework to identify and assess climatic factors contributing to infectious disease incidence in refugee communities.

Figure 1: The comprehensive macro-level data-driven nonparametric machine learning framework integrating data preprocessing, feature engineering, and model validation.

Methodology: The proposed framework integrates meta-heuristics feature selection algorithms (Particle Swarm Optimization and Genetic Algorithm) to select relevant variables from climate and health datasets. Time Variational Auto-Encoder (Time-VAE) is employed for synthetic data generation to address data scarcity challenges. SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) values are utilized to explain feature importance and its impact on model predictions with enhanced interpretability.

Diarrhea Outbreak Prediction - SHAP Feature Analysis

Feature importance analysis reveals that Minimum Temperature, Solar Radiation, and Sea Level Pressure are the most critical predictors for diarrhea outbreaks in refugee communities.

Figure 28: SHAP feature importance and summary plot for BART model (Diarrhea outbreak prediction). Left panel shows mean absolute SHAP values; right panel displays SHAP value distributions colored by feature magnitude.

Malaria Outbreak Prediction - SHAP Feature Analysis

Malaria prediction demonstrates strong sensitivity to Minimum Temperature, Relative Humidity at 2m, and Air Quality Index (AQI), reflecting vector ecology and transmission dynamics.

Figure 30: SHAP feature importance and summary plot for BART model (Malaria outbreak prediction). Shows how climatic variables interact to influence mosquito breeding and transmission rates.

Respiratory Tract Infection (RTI) Prediction - SHAP Feature Analysis

RTI prediction emphasizes Temperature at 2 Meters, Wind Speed at 2m, and Sunshine Hours as primary drivers, highlighting the importance of atmospheric conditions on respiratory pathogen transmission.

Figure 32: SHAP feature importance and summary plot for BART model (RTI outbreak prediction). Demonstrates the dominant role of temperature and wind patterns in respiratory infection dynamics.

Key Findings: This study finds significant improvement in predictive performance against limited sample size by using Variational Auto-Encoder as a data augmentation technique. Bayesian Additive Regression Trees (BART) best captures the complex nonlinear structure of climate-disease relationships. The framework successfully identifies climate-sensitive disease patterns and provides actionable insights for public health planning in resource-constrained settings.

Undergraduate Thesis

Industry 4.0 Readiness Assessment Framework and Barrier Analysis

Overview: This thesis presents a comprehensive framework for evaluating organizational readiness for Industry 4.0 digital transformation. The research identifies and prioritizes implementation barriers across multiple dimensions including technology, organization, human resources, and regulatory environment. Using hierarchical weighting analysis, the study provides a structured approach to assess maturity levels and guide strategic digital transformation initiatives.

Figure 7: The Readiness of Industry 4.0 under 7 Dimensions - comparative assessment across four companies showing varying maturity levels in People & Culture, Industry 4.0 Awareness, Organizational Strategy, Value Chain & Processes, Smart Manufacturing Technology, Product/Services Technology, and Industry 4.0 Base Technology.

Figure 6: The Seven Dimensions of Industry 4.0 Readiness Framework - comprising People & Culture, Industry 4.0 Awareness, Organizational Strategy, Value Chain & Processes, Smart Manufacturing Technology, Product/Services Technology, and Industry 4.0 Base Technology.

Methodology: The research employs Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) with weighted scoring to evaluate organizational readiness across seven critical dimensions. A comprehensive barrier analysis identifies 17 sub-barriers categorized under four major barrier types, with normalized global weights determining implementation priority.
